History and Evolution

Founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University, Google started as a search engine. It has since grown into one of the world's most influential tech companies, with products ranging from its Android operating system to its Google Cloud services. In 2015, Google underwent a major corporate restructuring and became a wholly-owned subsidiary of Alphabet Inc.

Key Technological Innovations

Company Culture and Influence on Innovation

Google's company culture is known for its emphasis on creativity and innovation. With its famous "20% time" policy, employees were encouraged to spend one day a week working on projects outside of their job descriptions, leading to products like Gmail and Google News. The company's campuses, known as Googleplexes, are designed to foster collaboration and creativity. Google's motto, "Don't be evil," underscores its commitment to ethical business practices, though it has faced criticism and challenges in this area.
